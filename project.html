Flagship Project

Tracing and Simulating Ideological Polarization in Bangladesh

ABM Simulation Visual Placeholder

Abstract

This project employs an Agent-Based Model (ABM) to simulate the emergence of ideological polarization in a multiparty system. By modeling individual agents with specific cognitive biases and social interaction rules, the simulation demonstrates how micro-level behaviors aggregate into macro-level political divides. The study specifically calibrates the model to the context of Bangladesh, exploring the dynamics of "hybrid regimes" and the erosion of democratic norms.

Key Findings

  • Ideological drift is accelerated by algorithmic filtering in social networks.
  • Multiparty systems are not immune to binary polarization under specific conditions of high inequality.
  • Agent interactions reveal a "tipping point" for radicalization.

Future PhD Work

I aim to extend this model by incorporating real-time social media data and more complex agent decision-making architectures (e.g., BDI agents) to predict potential conflict zones in fragile democracies.

Methods Used

  • Agent-Based Modeling (NetLogo)
  • Social Network Analysis
  • Monte Carlo Simulation
  • Python (Data Analysis)

Status
